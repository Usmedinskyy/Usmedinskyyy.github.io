Ryanair:Check-in experience redesign

Ryanair is Europe’s favourite airline, carrying 119m passengers on more than 1,800 daily flights in 33 countries. This amazing team delivers Europe’s No.1 on-time performance, and an industry leading 31-year safety record.

At some moment they had gotten a bit off-track with their website & mobile apps and our team at EPAM were invited to to fix this situation. I joined the team in 2014 as UX designer and worked alongside some of the smartest people I’ve met so far.

I worked with the Ryanair team in-house at Dublin for 4 months as UX designer.

My role

Service skills

I collaborated with the Head of Digital Experience at Ryanair on defining business goals of redesign & identifying key insights from company analytics.

I also crawled through user feedback from Ryanair’s support & public sources, and discovered some hidden questions & problems.

Prototyping

I built multiple interactive prototypes, including microinteractions & states for an efficient user testing and for better communication inside our team.

Design

I designed every aspect of the booking & check-in flows on the Ryanair website, from low-fi prototypes to development-ready assets, including animations.

Low-cost airlines don’t make money on tickets.

They make it on cross-sales while booking & check-in.

Discover phase

The first step was to determine really important user cases that drive client’s KPIs.

Through day-to-day interactions with stakeholders and current users, I collected information about booking & check-in process to understand pricing model, marketing patterns and special insights of an industry leading low-cost airline.

Research also contained thorough analysis of a competitive products. Consequently, I figured out clear and overall challenge for this task:

Challenge

To make check-in experience highlight relevant & affordable cross-sales, while increasing business KPIs, and ideally increasing user engagement.

With the few more important use cases crystalized, I turned my attention to real problems and built working prototypes of ideas as early as possible.

Personalized check-in

One of the key UX problems to be solved was clear: users were distracted with cross-sales & reported negative feedback about special Ryanair services, that was offered during online check-in.

Why not to present this routine like a well-crafted story with smart suggestions?

After many initial explorations (a few represented below), I realized that the service suggestions are a part of oftenly recurrent travel routines. You know this tasks very good — they are oftenly in your ‘to-do before travel’ list, like «rent a car», «add ski into my ticket booking», «renew my travel insurance», «find a cheap and cozy hotel» and others.

I explored a direction that personalizes check-in process with relevant service suggestions.

By reorganizing typical steps of check-in into a seamless story, we created a flow, that user see as a friendly assistant, which makes your check-in quicker and more personal, while following tight business requirements.

UI Design system

From the first prototypes, every design aspect of this task was my responsibility.

Starting from low-fidelity prototypes, sketches and user stories, I ended with a module UI system including detailed user flow, style guidelines, UI animation demo and specifications, content guidelines and more.

Ryanair’s new check-in flow have been brought to life with an overhauled UI, faster performance and hugely improved overall experience.

However, due to a critical development flaws, our goals were not achieved, and site was redesigned again with the help of another design team.

At the end, I learned how to connect business expectations and user needs into a beautiful and working solution.
